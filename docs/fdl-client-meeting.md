# FDL client meeting — "IDA 2030: Cliff or no cliff?"

Source material: FDL slide deck `IDA_GIZ_KAdequacyModel` (authors: Mathilde
Barras, Martin Kessler, Stephen Paduano — Finance for Development Lab, presented
at a GIZ meeting). Goal: turn it into an interactive scrollytelling report.

## Deck structure (as presented)

The deck already has a three-act arc that maps naturally onto report sections:

1. **IDA's growth**
   - "The largest fund for poor countries" (line chart)
   - "Even more when considering net transfers" (stacked bars)
   - "Fast growth institution" (combined chart)
   - "Balance sheet view" (bars)
2. **Financing IDA's growth**
   - "Commitment view" (bars)
   - "Equity's share is large… but declining" (bars)
   - "Equity growth" (stacked bars)
   - "Income view" (stacked bars)
3. **IDA's future**
   - "Reducing grants" (text slide)
   - "The objective" (line chart)

## Things to talk about

### Story & scope
- [ ] Is the report a 1:1 translation of this deck, or a chance to re-edit the
      story? The deck was written for a live GIZ audience — who is the report
      for (policy makers, G20 sherpas, researchers, press)?
- [ ] "Cliff or no cliff?" — keep as the report title? What is the one-sentence
      answer the reader should leave with?
- [ ] The three sections (growth → financing → future) work well for
      scrollytelling. Confirm this arc, and which single figure is the
      centerpiece of each section.
- [ ] Which slides drop entirely? ~10 charts is a lot for one scrolly report;
      report v1 carried roughly one animated figure per section beat.
- [ ] Is there a headline number for the landing (e.g. the size of the 2030
      financing gap / the "cliff")?

### Data
- [ ] Can they share the underlying data for every chart (Excel/CSV), not
      screenshots — including the capital adequacy model outputs the file name
      refers to?
- [ ] Data sources and citation lines per figure (World Bank/IDA annual
      reports? their own model?) — the report needs a source note on each chart.
- [ ] How current is the data, and will it need a refresh before publication
      (IDA21 replenishment numbers)?
- [ ] Projections in "The objective": which scenarios exist, and which do they
      want visualized (the deck's charts mix historical + forward-looking)?

### Text
- [ ] Who writes the report prose? Slide bullets ≠ report text — do they
      deliver copy, or do we adapt from the deck and they review?
- [ ] Language: English only, or also French? (FDL is Paris-based.)
- [ ] Author credits and any acknowledgements/funders to display.

### Design & brand
- [ ] Confirm the brand guidelines (Avril 2022) are still current.
- [ ] Kapra Neue Expanded webfont: confirm FDL's license covers use on this
      report's domain (files are self-hosted on findevlab.org today).
- [ ] The brand palette is muted — flag that charts will lean on direct labels,
      and extremes/emphasis will use the rust/terracotta accents. Show them the
      re-skinned prototype for sign-off on the direction.
- [ ] Cover image: current placeholder is the white FDL logo on brand slate. Do
      they have owned/licensed imagery for the cover, or budget to commission
      one? (Avoid unlicensed stock pulled from the web.)
- [ ] Author links: LinkedIn profile URLs for Mathilde Barras, Martin Kessler,
      Stephen Paduano (placeholders `#` in the landing until then).
- [ ] Logo placement (header? footer?) and whether SandraViz gets a credit line.

### Publishing & practicalities
- [ ] Where does it live: a link under findevlab.org (subdomain/embed) or the
      standalone Vercel link?
- [ ] Timeline: hard deadline (an event/replenishment date this should land
      before?), and their review loop (how many feedback rounds, single point
      of contact).
- [ ] Social preview (OG image), analytics they want, and whether the report
      should be indexable by search engines.
- [ ] Budget/proposal sign-off if this is still at proposal stage.
